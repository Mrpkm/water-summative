Anthropogenic Factors

How a growing population and rising demand drive water depletion — worldwide and in England.

❓What is it?

🌍 General

The world is facing problems because of its increasing population. Areas, where high saturation of agriculture and urban water usage is concentrated on few aquifers, water depletion can be faster than natural refillment. (Wada, Y., van Beek, L. P., van Kempen, C. M., Reckman, J. W., Vasak, S., & Bierkens) Even though areas can usually get water from other ground based sources such as rivers and lakes, regional differences can put immense pressure on specific areas of the world as in Sahel, South Africa, the Central U.S., Australia, India, Pakistan, and North-East China. (Wada, Y., van Beek, L. P., van Kempen, C. M., Reckman, J. W., Vasak, S., & Bierkens) Estimations include data that, global water depletion has increased by 224% and water water demand by 200% between the time period of 1960 to 2000. (Wada, Y., van Beek, L. P., van Kempen, C. M., Reckman, J. W., Vasak, S., & Bierkens)

📍 Local (England)

In England, water management has transformed from a fragmented approach, to a much more coordinated system. There exist 5 terms that have to be considered when were talking about water management, these include:”(1) allocation of water between competing uses, (2) increase supply, (3) water transfers from areas of surplus to areas of deficit, (4) reduce demand, and (5) plan use according to supply. “ (Leonard, A., Amezaga, J., Blackwell, R., Lewis, E., & Kilsby, C.) Over the years, the united kingdom has undergone changes, from using 27 local river authorities to EU legislation and national frameworks. The national framework phase has set out to meet the national needs, through drought resilience and reducing pressures on the environment. (Leonard, A., Amezaga, J., Blackwell, R., Lewis, E., & Kilsby, C.)

⚠️Problems

🌍 General

Our world has become dependent on groundwater resources. Studies suggest that, over 50% of domestic households rely on groundwater and over 40% of irrigation. (United Nations University.) Because of this, over 70% of all aquifers experience long term damage which are irreversible in human lifetime. As a result, the overall groundwater accessibility has seen a 62% decrease worldwide with only a 32% increase in accessibility. (United Nations University.)

📍 Local (England)

The climate change, population growth and environmental pressures all contribute to the massive water deficit of the UK. It is said that at 2055 the uk will be short 5 billion litres of water every day, with additional 1 billion litres of water for other uses. (UK government)

✅Solutions

A simple solution is to use more places to get water from. With over 40 years of data, scientists concluded that this is really beneficial for solving the groundwater depletion crisis. (Bittle, Jake) This is beneficial, because multiple sources can fill up faster than overusing just one source, here natural refillment is our friend not our enemy. The deficit in the UK is to be addressed by EA (Environmental Agency). EA expects around 60% of the depletion to be addressed by water companies managing demand and 40% from boosting supply and building new reservoirs. (UK government)
