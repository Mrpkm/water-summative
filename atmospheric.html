Atmospheric Factors

How the atmosphere moves and stores water throughout the water cycle.

🔑Key words

Atmosphere precipitation Atmospheric rivers Transpiration evaporation condensation water cycle

❓What is it?

🌍 General

Atmospheric factors, mainly influence, on how the water is moved/stored throughout the water cycle. For instance, through evaporation and later precipitation, almost 40,000 cubic kilometers of water is moved from the oceans to the continents. (Ambroggi, Robert P.) Even more fascinating, the atmosphere holds, at any given time, 12,900 cubic kilometers of water. (Water Science School.) Atmospheric factors, do this, by 3 processes. Transpiration, evaporation and condensation, each involve the atmosphere. (NOAA.) Over time, the 3 processes result in noticeable cloud patterns, particularly around the equator. These massive clouds carry water from all kinds of places to all kinds of places. In fact, by observing this with satellites technologies we can predict, where will be rain—commonly known as precipitation. (Ackerman, Steve & Platnick, Steven & Bhartia, P. & Duncan, Bryan & L'Ecuyer, Tristan & Heidinger, Andrew & Jackson, Gail & Loeb, N. & Schmit, T. & Smith, Nadia)

📍 Local (England)

Britain, experiences atmospheric drivers, primarily through atmospheric rivers. The so called:” Atmospheric rivers” are actually cloud patterns, which carry water in a length 2,000 km, on mid latitudes. (Staff, Carbon Brief.) These rivers bring water from the tropics, to mainland England, in the form of heavy rains. These rivers, especially seen with the el nino event (See more at global systems), cause massive floods in England around winter time. (Staff, Carbon Brief.)

⚠️Problems

🌍 General

Because of global warming, many places experience increased precipitation, whilst the ones, already deserted, experience even worse droughts. (Douville, Hervé, et al.) Oceans which are already warm, experience an even larger increase in evaporation—later resulting in higher precipitation—becasuse in response to the higher temperatures of the global warming, ice bergs melt, resulting in more water in the oceans, especially more water to be evaporated. (Douville, Hervé, et al.)

📍 Local (England)

This shows up a lot in England, especially when looked at the British isles. For instance, for more than 200 years, all of the wettest years have been in the 21st century. (Kendon, Mike, et al.) Even more, heating levels actually have shown a gradual decrease, dropping more than 500 c˙ Degreess in annual Heating Degree Days (HDD) (Kendon, Mike, et al.) This shows, that the years have become wetter, through floods, rain and other participation patterns, yet the people didn’t actually heat more. This means that people didn’t actually feel colder, yet the years have become wetter. This shows that the warmth, that made people feel less cold, increased precipitation, because it has also increased evaporation. But it also has to be noted, that the UK year, has split into 2 parts, one is drier and the other is more wet. (Kendon, Mike, et al.)

✅Solutions

A modern chemical treatment, called glaciogenic seeding, can even out the unbalanced precipitation, caused by global warming. This method consist of using chemicals, similar to the composition of ice, to artificially largen clouds, to result in raining. (Chen, Meilian, et al.) This method is effective in areas, where the precipitation actually decreased, because we could artificially increase the rain. Now, wouldn’t this make the year even more wet? Which wouldn’t solve the split year problem? Well studies show that the treatment can later reduce the rain, giving only temporary increased precipitation, and it can also reduce the height of the clouds, which results in a balanced water distribution from rain, through the year. (Chen, Meilian, et al.)
