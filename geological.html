Physical and Geological Factors

How geology stores water in aquifers and groundwater reserves — and what happens when they are over-used.

🔑Key words

— No key words were listed in this section of the original document.

❓What is it?

🌍 General

Geology can help us spot potential aquifers, and groundwater reserves. Aquifers, are underground water reservers, transmitting and storing water. (Fain, Jeremy.) This is an important factor, studied by experts—usually hydrogeologist–(Geologist who specializes in underground water reserves–the intersection of hydrology and geology–, studying porosity and permeability. Both factors are important to understand water access-. See more later… ), to use water effectively and avoid overexploitation of water. (Fain, Jeremy.) These reserves are the largest water reserves in the water cycle Ground water reserves can account for 3 meters in depth, if looked at continental averages. (Gleeson, T., Befus, K. M., Jasechko, S., Luijendijk, E., & Cardenas) Not only do cities, towns and even villages primarily rely on aquifers, but so do ecosystems. (UNESCO) This is why proper management of groundwater is beneficial and even decisive in water management. But we also have to keep in mind that global climate changes can dry these reserves drastically, making it difficult to access quality water. (UNESCO)

📍 Local (England)

The United Kingdom’s water reserves are experiencing pressure on its groundwater, and water reserves. With over 1.5 millions homes per year, the growing population demands more water. (Environmental Agency) As result, governmental acts are done to protect the wildlife, to then balance out the water usage.. (Environmental Agency) Several organisations have risen to solve the problem. They set out to advise the british government in water management. Specifically the British Geological Survey (BGS) has offered opportunities for groundwater scanning and other technical advice. (UKRI. “Groundwater - BGS Groundwater)

⚠️Problems

🌍 General

The overuse of groundwater is called groundwater depletion (The Groundwater Foundation). When groundwater is depleted, it usually happens because more water is used than what is refilled, sort of like a bank account: if you withdraw more than you can, you will not be able to buy that one snicker bar… In case, a groundwater source is depleted, there must be a place to get water from. So, cities must chose to get water from farther away, which causes more electricity usage, as the water has to be transferred at the same or even faster than before, at a bigger distance then before. (The Groundwater Foundation)

📍 Local (England)

The UK is experiencing severe droughts, which is said to create more than a billion litres of water deficit in south-east England by 2050. (© 2026 The South East Rivers Trust) The source states, that outliers like the droughts from 2004 to 2006 has set to become the norm by 2080. (© 2026 The South East Rivers Trust) It is also a problem that over 61% of the water supplying places are built before 1981 and over 13% which are more than 100 years old. (for, Department.) Even more, serious water pollution related accidents increased by 60% and over 5 billion water deficit is projected throughout Britain by 2055. (for, Department.)

✅Solutions

The UK government is actively trying to solve multiple problems at once, with its funding program. Currently new reservoirs are being built to reduce the groundwater usage in areas. (for, Department.) This will reduce the pressure on a few reservoirs because the increasement could even out the usage. This is important because in case the reservoir fails, multiple regions that rely primarily on it, would experience the shock. With multiple reservoirs however, this problem is solved, because the reservoirs can be used be different cities, or even multiple, to provide a steady stream of water flow. (for, Department.)
