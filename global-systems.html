Earth-scale ocean and atmosphere circulation that moves freshwater globally — and what it means for England.

❓What is it?

🌍 General

El nino (literal meaning is little boy) is an atmospheric circulation pattern, which moves warm air to parts of the world, whilst la nina (literal meaning is little girl) takes cool air to parts of the world. (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration.) El Nino can make significant changes in the weather. One of the biggest effects it has is changing the upwellings of the ocean. Normally, these bring up cold, nutrient rich water to the surface, but during el nino this process slows or stops altogether. (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration.) La Nina, pushed by the cold waters of the pacific, can lead to droughts in the USA. La nina can also contribute to the increased cold-water species, like squid and salmon to show up around California. (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration.)

📍 Local (England)

In the UK el nino can raise temperatures. Which as expressed earlier, can cause significant droughts, which put pressure on aquifers. (The National Centre for Atmospheric Science.) In the UK, La Nina can decrease temperatures. This can lead to either increased precipitation or snow, particularly in the northern regions. (Riley, Emily.)

⚠️Problems

🌍 General

So we know that the El Nino event can cause significant changes, floods, droughts and altered atmospheric conditions. However, scientists now predict an increased likelihood of a super el nino. (Parsons, Sarah.) The phenomenon occurs roughly every 10 to 15 years, but because of climate change, this years could have even more destructive effects. (Parsons, Sarah.) This event can potentially cause droughts, in the caribbean, central america, and northern brazil. (Parsons, Sarah.) This can put pressure on agriculture and water reservers and underground reservoirs. (Parsons, Sarah.) What is bad however, is that some regions are already under pressure, from the iranian-American conflict, making the situation even more threatening. (Parsons, Sarah.) On the other hand, El nino can cause increased precipitation and floods in regions like, Peru, eastern united states and parts of the middle east. This can potentially refill water reserves but not permanently. (Parsons, Sarah.)

📍 Local (England)

It is said that the Super El Nino can be devastating for Britain. The super el nino could be the fuel, to the burning fire of the global warming shaped earth. Millions if not billions or trillions can be lost in income globally due to crop failures. (Poynting, Mark, et al.)

✅Solutions

In the case of the 2026 super el Nino, or what it was in 2015 the “Godzilla El nino”, the protocol is the same for preparing. The red cross’ plan is to reduce the future consequences and the damages it could do. (The 2026 “El Niño)
