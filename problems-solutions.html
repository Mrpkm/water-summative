🌧️ Atmospheric Factors

Open full driver →

⚠️Problems

🌍 General

Because of global warming, many places experience increased precipitation, whilst the ones, already deserted, experience even worse droughts. (Douville, Hervé, et al.) Oceans which are already warm, experience an even larger increase in evaporation—later resulting in higher precipitation—becasuse in response to the higher temperatures of the global warming, ice bergs melt, resulting in more water in the oceans, especially more water to be evaporated. (Douville, Hervé, et al.)

📍 Local (England)

This shows up a lot in England, especially when looked at the British isles. For instance, for more than 200 years, all of the wettest years have been in the 21st century. (Kendon, Mike, et al.) Even more, heating levels actually have shown a gradual decrease, dropping more than 500 c˙ Degreess in annual Heating Degree Days (HDD) (Kendon, Mike, et al.) This shows, that the years have become wetter, through floods, rain and other participation patterns, yet the people didn’t actually heat more. This means that people didn’t actually feel colder, yet the years have become wetter. This shows that the warmth, that made people feel less cold, increased precipitation, because it has also increased evaporation. But it also has to be noted, that the UK year, has split into 2 parts, one is drier and the other is more wet. (Kendon, Mike, et al.)

✅Solutions

A modern chemical treatment, called glaciogenic seeding, can even out the unbalanced precipitation, caused by global warming. This method consist of using chemicals, similar to the composition of ice, to artificially largen clouds, to result in raining. (Chen, Meilian, et al.) This method is effective in areas, where the precipitation actually decreased, because we could artificially increase the rain. Now, wouldn’t this make the year even more wet? Which wouldn’t solve the split year problem? Well studies show that the treatment can later reduce the rain, giving only temporary increased precipitation, and it can also reduce the height of the clouds, which results in a balanced water distribution from rain, through the year. (Chen, Meilian, et al.)

🪨 Physical & Geological Factors

Open full driver →

⚠️Problems

🌍 General

The overuse of groundwater is called groundwater depletion (The Groundwater Foundation). When groundwater is depleted, it usually happens because more water is used than what is refilled, sort of like a bank account: if you withdraw more than you can, you will not be able to buy that one snicker bar… In case, a groundwater source is depleted, there must be a place to get water from. So, cities must chose to get water from farther away, which causes more electricity usage, as the water has to be transferred at the same or even faster than before, at a bigger distance then before. (The Groundwater Foundation)

📍 Local (England)

The UK is experiencing severe droughts, which is said to create more than a billion litres of water deficit in south-east England by 2050. (© 2026 The South East Rivers Trust) The source states, that outliers like the droughts from 2004 to 2006 has set to become the norm by 2080. (© 2026 The South East Rivers Trust) It is also a problem that over 61% of the water supplying places are built before 1981 and over 13% which are more than 100 years old. (for, Department.) Even more, serious water pollution related accidents increased by 60% and over 5 billion water deficit is projected throughout Britain by 2055. (for, Department.)

✅Solutions

The UK government is actively trying to solve multiple problems at once, with its funding program. Currently new reservoirs are being built to reduce the groundwater usage in areas. (for, Department.) This will reduce the pressure on a few reservoirs because the increasement could even out the usage. This is important because in case the reservoir fails, multiple regions that rely primarily on it, would experience the shock. With multiple reservoirs however, this problem is solved, because the reservoirs can be used be different cities, or even multiple, to provide a steady stream of water flow. (for, Department.)

🌍 Global Systems: El Niño

⚠️Problems

🌍 General

So we know that the El Nino event can cause significant changes, floods, droughts and altered atmospheric conditions. However, scientists now predict an increased likelihood of a super el nino. (Parsons, Sarah.) The phenomenon occurs roughly every 10 to 15 years, but because of climate change, this years could have even more destructive effects. (Parsons, Sarah.) This event can potentially cause droughts, in the caribbean, central america, and northern brazil. (Parsons, Sarah.) This can put pressure on agriculture and water reservers and underground reservoirs. (Parsons, Sarah.) What is bad however, is that some regions are already under pressure, from the iranian-American conflict, making the situation even more threatening. (Parsons, Sarah.) On the other hand, El nino can cause increased precipitation and floods in regions like, Peru, eastern united states and parts of the middle east. This can potentially refill water reserves but not permanently. (Parsons, Sarah.)

📍 Local (England)

It is said that the Super El Nino can be devastating for Britain. The super el nino could be the fuel, to the burning fire of the global warming shaped earth. Millions if not billions or trillions can be lost in income globally due to crop failures. (Poynting, Mark, et al.)

✅Solutions

In the case of the 2026 super el Nino, or what it was in 2015 the “Godzilla El nino”, the protocol is the same for preparing. The red cross’ plan is to reduce the future consequences and the damages it could do. (The 2026 “El Niño)

🏭 Anthropogenic (Human) Factors

Open full driver →

⚠️Problems

🌍 General

Our world has become dependent on groundwater resources. Studies suggest that, over 50% of domestic households rely on groundwater and over 40% of irrigation. (United Nations University.) Because of this, over 70% of all aquifers experience long term damage which are irreversible in human lifetime. As a result, the overall groundwater accessibility has seen a 62% decrease worldwide with only a 32% increase in accessibility. (United Nations University.)

📍 Local (England)

The climate change, population growth and environmental pressures all contribute to the massive water deficit of the UK. It is said that at 2055 the uk will be short 5 billion litres of water every day, with additional 1 billion litres of water for other uses. (UK government)

✅Solutions

A simple solution is to use more places to get water from. With over 40 years of data, scientists concluded that this is really beneficial for solving the groundwater depletion crisis. (Bittle, Jake) This is beneficial, because multiple sources can fill up faster than overusing just one source, here natural refillment is our friend not our enemy. The deficit in the UK is to be addressed by EA (Environmental Agency). EA expects around 60% of the depletion to be addressed by water companies managing demand and 40% from boosting supply and building new reservoirs. (UK government)
