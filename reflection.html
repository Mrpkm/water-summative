Science Reflection

G7 Unit 4 — Reflection on water scarcity, the teacher interview, and personal commitments to change.

❓Reflection questions & answers

1. During your formative, you analyzed how megacorporations like Coca-Cola or farming impact ecosystems, generate plastic pollution, and exploit water in vulnerable communities. Why is water scarcity not only a "natural problem" but also an ethical, economical and political one?

I feel it is more than just a natural problem, because corporations like Coca-Cola are reckless, not because they don’t know better, they are reckless because they are greedy. Coca-Cola’s business is ever expanding, but by maximizing expansion it risks creating environmental problems. This is a clear ethical problem, as they are sacrificing the good of the people for their own benefit. Coca-Cola specifically has lost multiple lawsuits, most of which are connected to water depletion, like the famous India water depletion in 2003. This shows that Coca-Cola had knowledge about their own wrongdoing, yet the want for power has undermined the moral weights of their actions.

2. How did your interview with the ISD teacher change your understanding of water distribution? Compare the water challenges of that country with the water reality you experience here in Debrecen, Hungary.

It changed my perspective of water distribution, because it has given me a clear plan about where to research and what. Hearing how some people unequally access water, and how even in countries which are infamous for rain, like Britain, still possess this problem made me curious on why this is happening. Hearing how policies and tax affect water usage made me guess and pinpoint my research on local trends. So overall, it was beneficial, because I heard terms which helped me continue and navigate my research.

3. While habits such as "turning off the tap while brushing your teeth, or taking shorter showers" are good, are they enough to solve a systemic water crisis?

In opposition to the popular belief, these habits are highly beneficial for saving water. For instance, the water counting program in Britain reduced the daily consumption of citizens by roughly 20 litres per day. (© 2026 The South East Rivers Trust) This is about 7 300 litres, or 45 oil barrels every year. (Convert Volume) If every UK citizen was able to do this change, they would save just about 496 billion litres of water a year, which is about 198 (WORLD AQUATICS) Olympic swimming pools. This amount of excess water would easily cover the water needed for economic water uses about 1.5 times or would cover a quarter of the water needed in 2055. This shows that small changes at large scale are very productive, even if we think that these actions are too small.

4. Based on your research on infrastructure (such as greywater systems, drip irrigation or xeriscaping) what is the difference between a small daily habit and a truly impactful solution?

Truly impactful approaches do not require citizen interaction. The UK government is planning to open new reservoirs which would be truly impactful. Here citizens don’t have to sacrifice water — reservoirs will do the work themselves through natural refillment. (Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs) These approaches solve the problems, and their solutions are scalable. Meaning, even if the demand increases the approach would still be successful. Scaling individual habits could even make things worse, because the water needs would lead to more pressure on the groundwater reserves, worsening refillment and causing a catalyst of overexhaustion.

5. Be completely honest: Has your daily behavior regarding water, food waste, or plastic consumption changed since we began this unit? If not, what is stopping you from making a real change?

I did not really change my consumption behavior that much, but I had become more aware of my actions. I use AI a lot for my programming projects. I had become recently aware of how much water AI uses. (Yañez-Barnuevo, Miguel) The changes I have implemented are: paying more attention to the sink being closed when I’m washing my teeth, and also started using more open-source AI’s so I’m not contributing to the expansion of the AI data centres.

6. How does your website raise awareness within the ISD community?

My website raises awareness because I’m using a lot of data on the problems. This will give an emotional reaction to the people. But to not make people think it is too late to change I have included a lot of solutions too. People will consider their actions twice before doing anything related to water consumption, thanks to my website.

7. Based on everything you have learned, researched, shared and heard from your interviewed teacher, write a realistic and high impact commitment that you will implement in your life to balance the water cycle and protect our environment.

I will keep being committed to implementing open-source AI in my life, so I’m not contributing to the rise of AI data centres, at least not on my part. So I can instantly cut short my own water consumption by a lot.
